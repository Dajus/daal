    4. inform employees which occupational health service provider will provide them with occupational health services.

  2. allow the worker to consult the records kept on him in connection with the provision of health and safety at work,

  5. ensure compliance with the prohibition of smoking in workplaces laid down by specific legislation.

The employer must keep records of the information and instructions.

The employer shall provide employees with training in legal and other regulations to ensure occupational safety and health, which shall complement their professional qualifications and requirements for the performance of work, which are relevant to the work they perform and relate to the risks with which the employee may come into contact at the workplace where the work is performed, and shall consistently require and monitor compliance with them. The employer shall provide the training referred to in the first sentence:

The employer shall be obliged to provide, at his own expense, technical and organisational measures for employees who are disabled, in particular the necessary adaptation of working conditions, adaptation of workplaces, the establishment of sheltered workplaces and workshops, the training or apprenticeship of such employees and the improvement of their qualifications in the performance of their regular employment.

Duties of employees

  • Attend an initial/periodic medical examination by a doctor providing occupational health services.
  • To start work always mentally and physically fit.
  • Not to consume alcoholic beverages or other addictive substances before and during the shift. Submit to alcohol and other drug testing by the employer and their designated supervisors.
  • Conduct themselves at work in such a way as not to endanger their own health or that of their co-workers, comply with the legislation on health and safety at work, the health and safety regulations and instructions issued by the employer, the principles of safe working and technological practices with which they have been familiarised (even a verbal instruction is a health and safety instruction) and not carry out any of the prohibited operations and manipulations. Prevent recklessness, negligence and malice.
  • ZEmployees are entitled to refuse to perform work which they reasonably believe to be an immediate and serious threat to their life or health or the life or health of others.
  • Always keep the safety of their actions in mind when working and do not overestimate their own abilities.
  • Not to operate a machine or equipment unless they have satisfied themselves that they will not endanger their own or another's health or life by doing so, or have been shown by a senior member of staff to be familiar with the machine or equipment.
  • Comply with the established prohibition against smoking in designated workplaces and workplaces where non-smokers are working.
  • Refrain from work for which they are not trained and which requires competence, e.g. working at heights.

Duties of employees

  • Refrain from operating appliances or equipment for which they are not trained or instructed.
  • Attend training and instruction organised by the employer in the interests of health and safety at work.
  • To keep the machinery, equipment, tools, instruments and workplace entrusted to them clean, tidy and in good working order.
  • Maintain order in the workplace.
  • Obey traffic, safety and fire signs and signs posted on the premises and observe warning signals and agreed signs, using designated pathways, passageways and communications.
  • Do not linger unnecessarily in hazardous areas (traffic routes, energy installations, server rooms, boiler rooms, etc.).
  • Have any accident properly treated and report the incident to your supervisor. The duty to report an accident also applies to witnesses to the accident. The employee is obliged to provide immediate first aid to the injured person and arrange medical treatment if necessary. In addition, prevent further injury by eliminating the source of danger. Witnesses to an accident at work are obliged to cooperate in clarifying the causes of the accident at work.
  • Report their condition to their supervisor in the event of health problems or sickness.
  • Wear protective equipment and prescribed personal protective equipment (PPE) at work, protect it from damage and take care of its routine maintenance - see PPE guidelines
  • Report immediately to the supervisor any deficiencies and defects that could endanger the safety and health of workers and contribute to their elimination to the best of their ability.

Personal protective equipment, work clothing and footwear, washing, cleaning and disinfecting agents and protective drinks § 104

  1. Where risks cannot be eliminated or sufficiently reduced by means of collective protection or work organisation measures, the employer shall provide employees with personal protective equipment. Personal protective equipment shall be protective equipment which must protect employees against risks, must not endanger their health, must not hinder the performance of their work and must meet the requirements laid down by special legislation.
  2. In an environment in which clothing or footwear is subject to extraordinary wear and tear or contamination at work or performs a protective function, the employer shall also provide work clothing or footwear as personal protective equipment.
  3. The employer shall be obliged to provide employees with washing, cleaning and disinfecting agents on the basis of the extent of contamination of the skin and clothing; in workplaces with unsatisfactory microclimatic conditions, to the extent and under the conditions laid down in the implementing legislation, also protective drinks.

Accidents at work

The law characterises an accident at work as a disorder (any damage) to health or death caused to an employee independently of his will by a short-term, sudden and violent action of external influences, in the performance of work tasks or in direct connection with it.

  • If an employee suffers an injury or death while performing his/her work duties or in direct connection therewith, this fact - an accident at work - must be clarified and recorded.
  • An accident at work that has caused an employee to be unable to work for more than 3 calendar days must be recorded on the record of the accident at work, sent to the Regional Labour Inspectorate (OIP) Ostrava, the employer's insurance company Kooperativa, the employee's insurance company, the trade unions if they are present in the company and the police if the accident was caused by someone else's fault.
  • The injured employee or a witness to the accident shall report his/her accident or the accident of another person to his/her supervisor, who shall carry out the initial treatment and make an entry in the accident book,
  • The employee shall not complete the accident book without the presence of the employee's supervisor.
  • If an accident occurs at work which has caused incapacity for work of less than 3 days (inclusive), it must also be entered in the accident book. Minor injuries, e.g. scratches, abrasions, are recorded in the accident book.
  • In the event of an accident at work with another employer, this must also be reported to that employer.
  • An accident at work is not an accident that occurs on the employee's way to and from work.

Hazardous chemicals

  • Hazardous chemical substances and mixtures (NCHLaS) shall always be stored in their original labelled and sealed containers.
  • Employees working with NCHLaS must be familiar with the safety data sheet for the NCHLaS in question
  • H and P phrases are found on the packaging or MSDS.
  • P-sentences are standardised instructions for the safe handling of chemicals and mixtures of chemicals.
  • H-phrases are standard hazard statements for chemicals and mixtures of chemicals.

Termination of employment § 48 - § 66 of the Labour Code

Employment may only be terminated by:

  • by agreement - if the employee and the employer agree, the employment relationship ends on the agreed date; the agreement must be in writing, otherwise it is invalid
  • by notice - the notice must be in writing, otherwise it is disregarded
    • notice given by the employee - even without giving a reason
    • notice given by the employer - only for lawful reasons:
      • if the employer or part of it is being closed down
      • if the employer relocates
      • for redundancy, organisational changes
      • according to a medical report, the employee may no longer perform his/her current job due to occupational disease or accident at work
  • immediate termination
  • dismissal during the probationary period
  • for a fixed period

Immediate termination of employment by "EMPLOYEE"

Exceptionally, the employer may only terminate the employment relationship immediately if:

  • if the employee has been finally convicted of a deliberate criminal offence and sentenced to an unconditional term of imprisonment of more than 1 year or to an unconditional sentence of at least 6 months for a deliberate criminal offence committed in the course of his/her work duties
  • if the employee has breached an obligation arising from the legal provisions relating to the work he performs in a particularly serious manner

The employer may not immediately terminate the employment relationship with:

  • a pregnant employee, an employee on maternity leave, or an employee on parental leave

Immediate termination of the employment relationship by an "EMPLOYEE"

An employee may only immediately terminate the employment relationship if:

  • the employer has not made it possible for the employee to perform other work suitable for him or her within 15 days from the date of the medical report (that he or she can no longer perform the work without serious risk to his or her health)
  • the employer has not paid the employee's wages or any part thereof within 15 days after the due date

The employer may not give notice to the employee or terminate the employment relationship with the employee immediately on the grounds of non-compliance with the temporary incapacity scheme.

Termination of employment during the probationary period

  • Both the employer and the employee may terminate the employment relationship during the probationary period without giving any reason.
  • However, the employer cannot terminate the employment relationship during the first 14 days of temporary incapacity for work.
  • Termination of the probationary employment relationship must be in writing
  • Upon termination of the employment relationship and the employment agreement, the employer is obliged to issue the employee with a certificate of employment. If the employee requests the employer to issue a work activity report, the employer must issue the report to the employee within 15 days.

Fire Protection - Duties of Employees

  • To act in such a way as to prevent fire, especially when using electrical, gas and other appliances, when storing and using flammable or fire-hazardous substances, handling them or handling open flames or other sources of ignition.
  • Comply with the conditions and instructions related to the fire safety of the products or activities that he or she performs.
  • Carry out orders and comply with prohibitions regarding fire protection at marked places.
  • Obtain a written order from an authorized senior employee when carrying out particularly fire-hazardous activities.
  • Observe the conditions for rapid fire fighting and rescue work.
  • Participate in fire protection training to the specified extent.
  • To create conditions for fire extinguishing and rescue work, in particular to maintain free passage widths of access roads, free escape routes, free access to electricity distribution facilities, water and gas shut-offs, material means of fire protection and fire safety equipment, etc.
  • Observe the technical conditions and instructions of machines, technical equipment, devices and tools related to fire safety.
  • Report any deficiencies found to the relevant managers.
  • In places with increased fire danger, follow the relevant fire regulations.

Fire Protection - Duties of Managers

  • Creates conditions for fire extinguishing and rescue work, through other employees they ensure the maintenance of free passage widths of access roads and access areas for fire fighting equipment, maintenance of free escape routes, free access to electricity distribution facilities, water and gas shut-offs, material means of fire protection and fire safety equipment, to connecting means, etc.
  • Comply with technical conditions and instructions relating to the fire safety of products or activities.
  • Indicate workplaces and other places with appropriate safety signs, orders, prohibitions and instructions in relation to fire protection, including places where material means of fire protection and fire safety equipment are located.
  • They regularly check compliance with fire protection regulations through the OZO in the Fire Department.
  • They cooperate with OZO in fire protection in all matters of fire protection.

Fire Protection - Duties of Managers

  • They provide the SPD body free of charge with products or samples necessary to carry out a fire technical expert to determine the cause of the fire.
  • Immediately notify the Fire Rescue Service of any fire occurring on the company's premises.
  • They control and direct the work of their subordinates in relation to the PO.
  • Participates in training of senior employees on FP.
  • They make sure that all workplaces they manage are left in a fire-safe condition at the end of working hours.

Beware of carbon monoxide (CO) poisoning

  • Carbon monoxide, also known as CO, is a very dangerous poisonous gas. Its danger is mainly due to its high toxicity and the fact that it does not smell, it is not colored in any way and has no taste. In the case of contact with this gas, there is a very rapid reduction in the transmission of oxygen in the blood, resulting in malaise, drowsiness and death. The affected person is usually unaware of their poisoning and remains in the affected area.
  • Carbon monoxide is produced by the poor combustion of solid, liquid and gaseous fuels such as gases, heating oils and coal or wood used for heating or hot water. Poor combustion usually begins to occur when there is a malfunction in the heating appliance or when there is insufficient air supply to the appliance.

Symptoms of carbon monoxide poisoning:

  • Mild poisoning: mild headache, stomach upset, exhaustion
  • Moderate poisoning: headache, vomiting, malaise, confusion, rapid heartbeat
  • Severe poisoning: unconsciousness, cardiac arrest, death
  • Carbon monoxide poisoning is indicated in the early stages by gradual disorientation and inability to leave the affected area or call for help. Children and pets tend to be affected earlier than adults. In case of sleep poisoning, the victim does not wake up.

Startup Instructions

The operation of the fire extinguisher is simple and the individual steps are clearly described directly on the bottle.

  1. Pull the fuse!
  2. Direct to the fire!
  3. Press the valve lever!

Intervention with a fire extinguisher during a fire must always be carried out in the direction of the wind and the ejection jet of extinguishing agent must always be directed to the source of the fire, or to burning objects - not to the flames, while it is necessary to constantly follow the fire. Keep a sufficient distance from fire, be careful not to transfer flames to undesirable places with the extinguishing agent jet or to get scalded when using water appliances. When extinguishing the fire, proceed from the bottom to the top (the flames usually go upwards) For powder fire extinguishers, it is recommended to extinguish intermittently. After extinguishing, be careful not to re-ignite.

Types of fire extinguishers

The correct choice of fire extinguisher is conditioned by the type of flammable substance. Depending on the type of flammable substance, we distinguish the so-called fire classes. The fire classes for which the fire extinguisher is intended are indicated on the bottle

  • Class A - fires of solid substances (e.g. wood, coal, textiles, paper, straw, hay, plastics), their burning is usually accompanied by glowing. Water and foam fire extinguishers are suitable for this class of fire.
  • Class B - fires of liquids or substances that turn into a liquid state (e.g. paints and varnishes, petrol, diesel, tar, oils, thinners, acetone, waxes, asphalt, resins, lubricants). For this class of fire, foam and powder fire extinguishers are suitable.
  • Class C - gas fires (e.g. natural gas, propane-butane, hydrogen, town gas, acetylene, methane). For this fire class, powder or gas fire extinguishers with CO2 (carbon dioxide) filling are suitable.
